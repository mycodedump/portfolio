FEATURE DESIGN · ICICI BANK

iTravel

Fixing a 30% international transaction decline rate on ICICI credit cards.

Role Sole UX/UI Designer
Team PM, Engineering, Risk & Compliance
Status With client, pre-launch
[ hero screen ]

The problem

ICICI credit cards decline 30% of international transactions. Not a card-network issue — by RBI mandate, every card ships with international usage off by default, reversible only by the user, manually, inside the app.

Result: a customer taps their card abroad, gets declined, has no idea the setting exists.

30%

international transactions declined

1

sole designer, end to end

3 HMWs guiding the work

1. Make activation easy and confidence-building
2. Get genuine transactions approved seamlessly
3. Give users one view for all international travel issues

Why "just surface the toggle" isn't the fix

RBI requires separate toggles for POS, ATM, and e-commerce. A user enabling POS still gets silently declined buying online. On top of that, the fraud engine checks transaction plausibility, not just whether the toggle is on.

Real problem: the bank has no signal a customer is traveling until they're already declined. iTravel closes that gap before the trip starts.

Key decisions

Decision 1

Multi-channel activation, not one screen

Most users barely open iMobile anymore — bill payments increasingly happen via CRED. An in-app-only entry point reaches almost nobody. Built activation across dashboard, manage cards, plus Instagram and email campaigns, branching by install state and whether the user already holds a card.

The drawer is a forced interstitial, deliberately — the user clicked an ad specifically for iTravel, so a dashboard detour would be the actual friction.

Decision 2

Layover capture for fraud plausibility

The brief asked for destination + dates. I added layover/transit stops — my proposal, not in the brief. Fraud engines flag transactions that look geographically implausible. A declared trip — India → Singapore (layover) → Japan — gives the engine a trail to match, so the Tokyo swipe reads as expected, not anomalous.

Decision 3

Live conversion rate on the limit slider

Card control limits shown as a slider with a live conversion-rate label for the destination currency — removing mental currency math, preventing under-set limits that look fine in INR but aren't, and surfacing a natural credit-limit-increase upsell when intended spend exceeds the current limit.

Decision 4

Three notification channels, not one

Travelers swap to local SIMs or data-only eSIMs abroad. One default number becomes a single point of failure for OTP delivery. Users choose between email, WhatsApp, or an international number — email also persists as a searchable record long after the trip ends.

Decision 5

Auto-expiring controls

RBI mandates manual activation but says nothing about deactivation — left alone, people forget to turn it off. Proposed international access auto-expiring when the declared trip ends, shrinking the fraud window to exactly the trip and removing the "am I committing forever" hesitation.

Decision 6

A summary screen, not a dead end

Active trips show as a live card on the dashboard. Post-trip: a Spotify-Wrapped-style summary — spend recap, country stamps, entry into travel history. Turns a one-time setup flow into something worth returning to.

Status

Design finalized through multiple iteration rounds, currently with the client for review. Not yet shipped — no post-launch data exists yet. The number this is designed against is still the 30% decline it set out to fix.
